Rethinking Education:

On Indigenous Land

Chloe Ierullo 500684169 & Daehee Jang 500707183

GEO605 Jeanne Maurer

Abstract

Throughout past Indigenous experiences, Canada had a long history of exploiting these communities. One of the major issues brought upon Indigenous peoples was insufficient education, which is available to all individuals in Westernized communities. Education is a basic right in Canada therefore should be available to all, including Indigenous peoples. However, the issues today and in the past contradict this law. The education provided to Indigenous communities are not practical or respectful of their cultural and spiritual traditions. By implementing a higher level education located on Indigenous land, it would provide sovereignty to Indigenous communities. This project analyzes the approach of an ecologically and culturally sustainable higher level online education, provided throughout all Indigenous communities across Canada. This model is reliant on the continuation of federal funding and organization of Indigenous peoples education. This online education model is a tailored curriculum incorporating Indigenous culture and spiritual tradition by recognizing the right to self-determination of Indigenous peoples. This model focuses on the political, cultural and environmental aspects of how this online education platform provides Indigenous communities with the power to become a nation within a nation while being self-sufficient and not as reliant on the Crown.
