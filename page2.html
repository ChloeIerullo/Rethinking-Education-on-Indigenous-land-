Reflecting on: Simpson and Elliot's Article's

While Simpson and Elliot have quite contradicting approaches to how Indigenous peoples and the Government should move forward, our education model takes into consideration aspects from both arguments.

Simpson argues that the Indigenous peoples should assimilate to Western culture and quit their traditional ways. He claims that they are too reliant on the Crown and should contribute to the rest of society. His point of view focuses on the economic value and lack of it.

Our model incorporate his ideology to an extent. Having higher level education available to Indigenous communities will give them the opportunity to be self sufficient and eventually non-reliant on the Crown. This educational opportunity will give them the resources needed to go back to a non-waged based community in this changing ecological environment.

Elliot argues that the only solution of the exploitation of Indigenous peoples is creating sovereignty within the Indigenous community. Canada must recognize the right to self-determination and take note of the sovereignty of Indigenous peoples.
